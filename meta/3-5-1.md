national_indicator_description: 'This indicator measures the proportion of Canadians meeting the Canadian physical activity guidelines. <br><br>
Canadian 24-Hour movement guidelines for 3 to 4 year olds consist of accumulating at least 180 minutes spent in a variety of physical activities spread throughout the day, of which at least 60 minutes is energetic play—more is better.<br><br>
Canadian 24-Hour movement guidelines for 5 to 17 year olds consist of accumulating at least 60 minutes of moderate- to vigorous-intensity physical activity daily. Vigorous physical activities, and muscle and  bone strengthening activities should each be incorporated  at least 3 days per week.<br><br>
Canadian 24-hour movement guidelines for adults 18 to 64 year olds consist of accumulating at least 150 minutes of moderate- to vigorous-intensity aerobic physical activity per week as well as muscle strengthening activities using major muscle groups at least twice a week.<br><br>
Canadian 24-hour movement guidelines for adults 65 to 79 year olds consist of accumulating at least 150 minutes of moderate- to vigorous-intensity aerobic physical activity per week as well as muscle strengthening activities using major muscle groups at least twice a week and physical activities that challenge balance. <br><br>
The guidelines can be found in Canadian Society for Exercise Physiology. Canadian 24-Hour Movement Guidelines. Ottawa, ON. 2020. <a href="https://csepguidelines.ca/">https://csepguidelines.ca/</a>'
